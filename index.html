Nutrition System

What is Nutrition?

Think of Nutrition as the battery power for crafting stations in Albion Online. Every time you craft or refine an item, the station "eats" some food to get the job done. If the station runs out of nutrition, it stops working until the owner feeds it.

How to Calculate Nutrition Cost

The amount of nutrition a station uses depends entirely on the Item Value of what you are making.

The Simple Formula:

Nutrition Consumed = Item Value × 0.1125

Item Value: A fixed number found at the bottom of any item's info window (click the item in your inventory to see it).

0.1125: The magic number used by the game to convert value into fuel cost.

Example Calculation

If you are refining Tier 4 Worked Leather:

1
Find Item Value: A piece of T4 Leather has an Item Value of 6.
2
Apply Formula: 6 × 0.1125 = 0.675
3
Result: Every time you refine one T4 Leather, the station loses 0.675 units of nutrition.

Why does this matter?

Shop owners in cities charge you a silver fee based on this number. They usually set a price per 100 units of nutrition used. If a shop tax is 500 Silver, you aren't paying for the item—you are paying for the "fuel" it took to make it.
