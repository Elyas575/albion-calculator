Why use this calculator?
Every time you refine materials, the game gives a percentage of them back to you. That's your return bonus. Most players never account for it and end up calculating their costs wrong, either underpricing and losing silver or overpricing and not selling fast enough. This tool figures out what you actually spend per item once the bonus is factored in. That number is your real floor. Price above it and you profit. Price below it and you lose.
Here is a real example of how this plays out. Buy raw hide in Bridgewatch. Take that hide to Martlock and refine it there. Martlock has a city bonus for leather refining so your return rate is higher, which means your true cost per piece is lower. Then check the market in other cities and find one where refined leather is selling above your true cost. Sell there. The difference is pure profit and you know the exact number before you even start.

Works for all items
This is just one example. Try to find any other item you want - this method works for all non-artifact items. Artifact items don't get returned to you as the bonus, so they're not calculated in the return rate system.
Step by step
1
Fill in the Materials tab
Pick one item you want to refine. Open the recipe in-game and enter each raw material, the quantity required, and its current market price. Add a row for every ingredient in the recipe.
2
Set your return rate
Drag the slider to your refining return bonus. You can see this number in-game by hovering over the crafting station before you craft. It changes based on your city, your focus, and any bonuses you have active.
3
Read your true cost
The True Cost card is the number you need. That is the silver you actually spend per item after the game returns materials to you. Do not sell below this number. Everything above it is profit.
4
Use Scale for bulk runs
If you are planning to refine a large batch, go to the Scale tab. It shows your total spend at 100, 500, 1,000 and 5,000 items, or any number you type in. Good for checking whether a session is worth the capital before you commit.
5
Check the Profit tab before you sell
Look up the sell price in your target city and enter it in the Profit tab along with how many items you plan to sell. It shows you profit per item, total profit, and your margin. If the number is red your sell price is too low and you need to find a better market.
6
Add station fees in the Nutrition tab
If you use a player-owned station they charge you per craft based on how much nutrition your item consumes. Open the Nutrition tab, enter the item value from the in-game item window and the station's price per 100 nutrition, and it tells you the exact fee per craft so you can add it to your true cost.
What each tab does
Materials
Your main workspace. Enter the recipe for one item, set your return bonus, and get your true cost. Every other tab pulls from this one so always fill it in first.
Scale
Multiplies your true cost by a quantity. Preset options for 100, 500, 1,000 and 5,000 items plus a custom field. Use it to know your total capital requirement before starting a big batch.
Profit
Enter a sell price and quantity to see profit per item, total profit, revenue, cost and margin. Green means you make money. Red means you lose it. Adjust the sell price or find a better city until the numbers work.
Nutrition
Calculates the fee a player-owned crafting station charges you per craft. The formula is Item Value times 0.1125 to get nutrition consumed, then divided by 100 and multiplied by the station's rate. Tells you the exact silver cost so you can account for it in your pricing.
