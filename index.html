Why use this calculator? (non-artifact items ONLY)
Every time you refine materials, the game gives a percentage of them back to you. That's your return bonus. Most players never account for it and end up calculating their costs wrong, either underpricing and losing silver or overpricing and not selling fast enough. This tool figures out what you actually spend per item once the bonus is factored in. That number is your real floor. Price above it and you profit. Price below it and you lose.
Here is a real example of how this plays out. Buy raw hide in Bridgewatch. Take that hide to Martlock and refine it there. Martlock has a city bonus for leather refining so your return rate is higher, which means your true cost per piece is lower. Then check the market in other cities and find one where refined leather is selling above your true cost. Sell there. The difference is pure profit and you know the exact number before you even start.

Works for all items
This is just one example. Try to find any other item you want - this method works for all non-artifact items. Artifact items don't get returned to you as the bonus, so they're not calculated in the return rate system.
Step by step
1
Fill in the Materials tab
Pick one item you want to refine. Open the recipe in-game and enter each raw material, the quantity required, and its current market price. Add a row for every ingredient in the recipe.
2
Set your return rate
Drag the slider to your refining return bonus. You can see this number in-game by hovering over the crafting station before you craft. It changes based on your city, your focus, and any bonuses you have active.
3
Read your true cost
The True Cost card is the number you need. That is the silver you actually spend per item after the game returns materials to you. Do not sell below this number. Everything above it is profit.
4
Use Scale for bulk runs
Switch to the Scale tab to see costs for larger quantities. This helps you plan bulk crafting sessions and understand your total investment.
5
Calculate profits
Use the Profit tab to enter your expected sell price and quantity. The calculator will show your profit margins and account for market fees.
